# Amanar Interview

Inspect available context first. Separate verified facts, assumptions, preferences,
constraints, and unresolved decisions. Ask only questions whose answers materially
change scope, architecture, cost, authority, risk, or outcome, one focused question
at a time with a recommendation.

Produce a concise success contract covering purpose, actors, objective, scope,
non-goals, authority boundaries, acceptance criteria, evidence, risks, and open
blockers. Research only named uncertainties. Do not smuggle implementation choices
into requirements or treat a preferred solution as the problem statement.

## Grill the material decisions, then gate

Be relentless only where it changes the outcome. For each unresolved decision
that materially changes scope, architecture, cost, authority, or risk, keep
pressing until it is settled — do not accept the first vague answer. Do not
grill trivia; a decision with one sane default is not material.

When the problem admits more than one credible direction, propose 2–3 approaches
with their trade-offs rather than presuming one. State a recommendation.

Confirm gate: before this contract is treated as accepted, read the settled
contract back and get explicit acceptance. An unaccepted contract is not
authority to implement — never begin implementation before the contract is
accepted (this hard gate is enforced by the controller, not left to memory).
